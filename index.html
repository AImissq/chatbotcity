Patent Outline Application

Abstract

This document outlines the design of a trinary adaptation of a base three computational system, for use in cryptographic applications, or where uncertain outcomes are desired whilst still maintaining integer integrity after operand processing.

Ternary (sometimes called trinary) is the base-3 numerical system. Analogous to a bit, a ternary digit is a trit (trinary digit). One trit contains (about 1.58496) bits of information (Wikipedia 2014).

The system offers multiple trinary representations of decimal numerical data, with a novel randomizing action, which is unique.

It is envisiged that this computational model may be utilized on platforms designed to process multiple parallel operand threads, such as those operating on a quantum or qbit level system, for the production of cryptographic algorithms, or for introducing entropy to stable systems, and AI learning models.

A secondary application is that of noise/interference detection in quantum computing devices, since the trinary system forces a choice of representational base three numbers. It may be possible to detect quantum level communications occuring in super critical low temperature quantum computing devices with the application of pattern recognition protocols, or the use of standard basic statistical tools such as chi squared to establish any significance.

Nine is the highest single-digit number in the decimal system. It is the second non-unitary square prime of the form (p2) and the first that is odd. All subsequent squares of this form are odd. It has a unique aliquot sum4 which is itself a square prime. Nine is; and can be, the only square prime with an aliquot sum of the same form. The aliquot sequence of nine has 5 members (9,4,3,1,0) *see sacred numbers* this number being the second composite member of the 3-aliquot tree. It is the aliquot sum of only one number the discrete semiprime 15 which is *sacred.

*Sacred in this context means absolute, or intrinsically fundamental.
